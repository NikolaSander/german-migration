About the Data

Our German Internal Migration (GIM) database holds annual migration counts drawn from the German population register for the period 1995 to 2010. Directional migration flows are provided for two geographies with different levels of complexity: 397 Temporally-Consistent Counties (TCCs) and 132 so-called "analytical regions" (ARs). The data are directly comparable across time and capture the number of moves across county boundaries over 12-month periods. See this article published in Comparative Population Studies for more details.

Data available in the article's online Appendix.

About the plot

You don't need a map to show migration flows! Inspired by Martin Krzywinski's Circos, I developed a visualisation that makes the complex migration patterns more accessible to the human eye. The circular plot shows directional flows between 134 Temporally-Consistent Counties that recorded total movements (inflows + outflows) of more than 12,000 people in 2010. Only flows containing at least 1,000 migrants are shown. The window that pops up when hovering over the plot indicates the annual number of inflows and outflows between the 134 counties.

German internal migration in 2010

The graphic depicts flows between 15 origins and destinations in 2010. These are the seven largest German cities plus Leipzig/Dresden, and seven regions with all remaining counties. Only flows containing at least 6,000 migrants are shown.

How to read the plot

How to read the plot

Origins and destinations are represented by the circle’s segments. Each region/county is assigned a colour. Flows have the same colour as their origin and the width indicates their size. The direction of the flow is also shown by the gap between flow and county/region: small gap indicates origin; large gap indicates destination. Click on a segment to switch between county and region view.

Switch between time periods using Ctrl and number keys (1,2,3,4); reset the plot using Ctrl and r; zoom in/out with Ctrl and +/-
